The Ashes

In 2015 England and Australia will contest the 69th Ashes series, Australia having won 32 of their previous encounters and England 31. Australia were most dominant between 1989 and 2002, when they snaffled eight series on the spin, while England won 11 of the first 12 contests, all of them in the 19th century. Over just the last 100 years Australia have a greater 25-16 lead but for all their sometime superiority, this cricketing rivalry remains red-hot.

2005 - 15

Kevin Pietersen was the leading run-accumulator on either side, just edging out Michael Clarke and with an average better by 0.07 runs. In terms of averages alone the most successful batsman was Australia’s Michael Hussey, who was running along at 59.27 between 2005 and his retirement in 2013.

1989 - 2003

Steve Waugh scored nearly 1000 runs more than any Englishman in this period (963, to be precise), with Mark Taylor and Mark Waugh (whose average of 60.91 was considerably better than his brother’s, or anyone else’s of note) also outscoring England’s top performer, Mike Atherton, by at least 300

1977 - 1987

David Gower, England’s most successful post-war Ashes batsman, is the leader of this field, edging out Alan Border (who played one innings fewer).

2005 - 15

England’s James Anderson is the leading wicket-taker on either side, though he has taken 23 matches to accumulate his 77 scalps, where Mitchell Johnson has raced to 72 in 14 Tests. Shane Warne is the joint fourth highest wicket-taker from just 10 Tests, having last played against England in 2007

1989 - 2003

Shane Warne and Glenn McGrath unsurprisingly stand head, shoulders, knees and toes above their rivals, England’s torturers-in-chief weighing in with a combined 249 wickets. England’s top two, Darren Gough and Andy Caddick, took a comparatively meagre 138 between them.

1977 - 1987

Ian Botham, England’s second most prolific batsman in this period, is also the best bowler by a distance, with 145 wickets. His closest competition comes from Bob Willis, who managed 99, with Dennis Lillee’s 82 Australia’s best. England also had the most successful spinner, in the shape of John Emburey; Australia’s best of the time, though in terms of strike rate Australia’s Bruce Yardley was not far behind, averaging just one more delivery bowled per wicket taken
